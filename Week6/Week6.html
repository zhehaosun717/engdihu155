What is a 'theme' in literature?

"Theme" LitCharts, 30 January 2026

A theme is a universal idea, lesson, or message explored throughout a work of literature. One key characteristic of literary themes is their universality, which is to say that themes are ideas that not only apply to the specific characters and events of a book or play, but also express broader truths about human experience that readers can apply to their own lives.

For example, John Steinbeck's The Grapes of Wrath (about a family of tenant farmers who are displaced from their land in Oklahoma) is a book whose themes might be said to include the inhumanity of capitalism, as well as the vitality and necessity of family and friendship.

Write an essay that consists of:

  • An introduction with a strong, argumentative thesis, clear roadmap, and "so what" statement.
  • ~3 critical paragraphs with claims/sub-topic sentences that undertake your analysis.
    • The structure of the paragraph should use a topic sentence, evidence-analysis pairs, and statement of significance as we learned in class.
    • Statements of significance in the essay should say why the paragraph is important for your argument.
    • Well framed use of primary evidence and analysis.
  • A 4-5 sentence conclusion that expands from your essay's argument to broader significance within the themes of the course.

Essays should be no more than 1000 words.

Three Argumentative Body Paragraphs

These are NOT 'They Say' / 'I Say' / 'Expansion' paragraphs

Remember – each paragraph should contain one clear argument, relating to one theme (row) in your evidence chart.

  • 1 strong and accurate topic sentence/claim for your paragraph.
  • 4-5 well-written, observant sentences.
    • At least 1 well-chosen, framed, and grammatically integrated quotation.
    • Discussion or explanation at least as long as the quotation.
  • 1 final sentence that expands on the summary.

*This is not the same thing as a "connector" sentence. Each paragraph should represent a single theme or row in your evidence chart.

Key Features of a Thesis Statement

  • It should be arguable. One should be able to reasonably disagree with it.
  • Its scope should be adequate with regard to the length of the essay.
  • It should express one central idea.

Some Tips:

  • Use a confident tone
  • Know where to put your thesis
  • Make it the right length

What Do You Say in Your Thesis?

I am arguing X based on the reasoning Y

Example:

"The metanarrative in The Book of Phoenix mirrors the protagonist's journey of rebirth by beginning and ending in a post-technology world."

A reader should be able to predict what you will be discussing in your essay just by reading the thesis statement.

The Book of Phoenix - Passage

"One must stop time to listen to a story. The storyteller starts it again. She starts it in her own place, in her own moment, in her own point of view. As long as you listen, she is in charge of your destiny. You and the storyteller share everything, even your existence.

Listen…

I started picking up the local accent. I could never fit in, so that wasn't the reason. I just liked the sound of it. I missed my love Saeed, my friend Mmuo, the other Tower 7 prisoners who weren't so nice to me. Speaking like the Ghanaians reminded me of them all. Plus, after all that had happened, it felt good to be different from what I had been, yet the same. That which was me would never change. That which was me could survive death. Over and over.

I was Phoenix."
